How We Got Here

From starting elementary school to completing high school, I wasn't ever sure exactly what I wanted to do in life. All I knew was to study more and more to consistently achieve better grades. This was all just to please my family. There wasn't much sense of purpose just yet.

Ever since starting my college applications during my senior year of high school, however, with the guidance from my two brothers, I was sure I wanted to pursue a degree and career in computer science. Honestly, it didn't take much to decide on what I wanted to do -- just some Youtube videos showcasing the endless possibilites of software development.

Throughout college I was content, to say the least, about my decision back in high school. I'd like to take this time to thank my past self. With the knowledge and skills that I've gained from most courses I can do more than I could have ever imagined before! So, now we're here. I write about my life in hopes to contribute what I've learned throughout the years and gain further insight to a platform.

A Passion of Mine

Throwback to middle school I realized that playing video games and cycling weren't the only forms of entertainment. It was then that I discovered an interest for volleyball. I wasn't exposed to the sport from social media or anything. This was just a result of brainstorming fun activities after school with my friends.

It didn't take long for this interest to turn into a passion and from passion to love. I felt a rush everytime the ball made contact with my hand and the sensation of "rolling" the ball over the net was just so addictive. At this point I was fairly new to the sport and knowing that I could be so much better gave me a strong sense of motivation to continuously practice.

Fast forward to high school I maintained my love for volleyball. I would say that bumping and setting a volleyball became second-nature. I often looked back to see how much I improved which consistently fueled my urge to get even better. I believe this drive to improve rubbed on my peers and teammates, earning me the title as varsity captain. Ever since then, I have been playing from time to time, more leisurely, in open gyms.

Serving my Community

My hometown is a place that I will forever indebted to for the love and kindness that its community holds. In order to pay them back, I enrolled in my school's Air Force JROTC program and some volunteering groups outside of school including Quincy Asian Resources Inc. With these groups of individuals who shared a similar goal as me, I was able to serve and give back to my community.

The AFJROTC program hosted events that amounted to over 1000 hours of community service. Throughout my four years as a cadet, I was lucky enough to experience both the satisfaction of helping groups of people, as well as leading service groups.
